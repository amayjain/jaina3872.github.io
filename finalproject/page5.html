Table

Type of Black Hole Formation Mass
Stellar Black Holes - gravity overcomes the nuclear processes' pressure
- center implodes as collapse of dense mass occurs
- seen by presence of accretion disk (gas around black hole)		 3-10 times that of the sun
Supermassive Black Holes - forms the same way as stellar black holes
- mass accumulation from drawing in of fast moving gas and extreme gravitational forces acting on it
- formed during early stages of galaxy arrivals		 105-1010 times that of the sun
Miniature Black Holes -might have formed during Big Bang
- so much matter was rapidly expanding that some of it might have been compressed
- event horizons can be as small as the width of atomic particles		 2-10 times that of the sun
