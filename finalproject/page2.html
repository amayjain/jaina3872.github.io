Black Hole Formation/Vocabulary

How do black holes form?

Step 1: The first step is to look at a star and understand that nuclear fusion processes are occuring inside the core of the star.

Step 2: New elements are being made and energy is being released exerting pressure outwards.

Step 3: However, gravity is pushing inwards onto the star causing it to collapse.

Step 4: This process keeps on occuring until the star has reached its limit on fusions inside the core and is exhausted.

Step 5: The star then becomes a white dwarf which is the remnant of the previous star, but with much more mass densely packed into a small volume.

Step 6: Gravity is the dominant force and the core in the white dwraf cannot repel it, so it collapses on itself once again.

Step 7: This time around the core is no longer there and in its place is a region with infinite volume.

What are the different parts of a black hole?

Black Hole - The black hole itself is a region in space where no matter can escape it as the pull of gravity is very intense.

Singularity - This is the part in a black hole where all matter is squished down to no volume and infinite density.

The Event Horizon - This the border around a black hole where anything past this area will automatically be pulled into the black hole. It is commonly known as the point of no return.

The Schwarzschild Radius - This is the radius of the event horizon where the escape velocity is equal to the speed of light.

Accretion Disk - This appears to be a cloud around the black hole composed of matter that keeps on feeding the black hole continuosly.

Ergosphere - It is a region located just outside the blackhole that rotates space-time as the black hole's mass rotates.
